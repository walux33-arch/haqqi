The Problem

Legal knowledge is locked
behind a language barrier

📜

Inaccessible Language

Moroccan laws are written in Fusha Arabic and French. Only 20% of Moroccans understand these — the remaining 80% speak Darija exclusively.

💰

Costly Consultations

A single lawyer consultation costs 500–2,000 MAD. For 60% of Moroccans living on less than 5,000 MAD/month, this is prohibitive.

🔍

No Trusted Digital Alternative

Existing legal websites are in French/Arabic, outdated, or run by unqualified sources. There's no AI platform rooted in Morocco's official legal corpus.

⚠️

Misinformation Risk

Moroccans turn to social media (Facebook groups, WhatsApp) for legal advice — often receiving incorrect or dangerous information.
